✍️ Poem — English & Nepali

In Kathmandu, a gentle breeze stirs,

carrying scents of dampened earth and leaves.

The sky is veiled, with clouds that bring

a soothing calm, a respite from heat's dark sleeves.

In Biratnagar, the sun beats down,

warmth that clings, a heavy, languid air.

Aalborg's chill is crisp, with winds that cut,

and clouds that shroud, a somber, greyish lair.

But if I had to choose, I'd wander in Kathmandu's mild light,

where the breeze whispers secrets, and the heart takes flight.
