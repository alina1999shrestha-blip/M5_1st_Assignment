In Kathmandu, a gentle breeze whispers low,

as sunshine warms the streets with a gentle glow.

In Biratnagar, the air is thick with heat,

a sweltering haze that wraps the city tight.

Aalborg's chill is palpable, a dampness that clings,

with grey skies weeping tears of misty rain that brings.

The scent of wet earth rises, a primal, earthly smell,

in Aalborg's somber skies, while Kathmandu's warmth, I'd dwell.
