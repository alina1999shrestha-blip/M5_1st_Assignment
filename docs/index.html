✍️ Poem — English & Nepali

In Kathmandu, the misty veil of morning clings,

as whispers of wind carry scents of dampened earth that brings

a soothing calm, a gentle hush that wraps the city tight.

In Biratnagar, the sun beats down, a fiery blade that cuts the air,

with warmth that sears the skin, and light that dances, wild and fair.

Aalborg's chill begins to seep, a creeping frost that nips the nose,

as grey skies weep with sorrow, and the breeze whispers winter's woes.

The warmth of Biratnagar's breeze, though fierce, may not be kind,

yet Kathmandu's gentle morning, with its misty veil, I'd find

more welcoming, a softer fold, where heart and soul can unwind.

In Kathmandu, the air is sweet, with a gentle touch that's hard to leave.
