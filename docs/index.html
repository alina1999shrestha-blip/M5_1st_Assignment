✍️ Poem — English & Nepali

In Kathmandu's misty veil, the air is soft and warm,

With scents of wet earth rising, a gentle, soothing form.

Biratnagar's breeze whispers secrets, as the sun beats down,

A fiery kiss that brings life to the parched, brown ground.

Aalborg's chill begins to seep, with winds that cut like glass,

As clouds congregate, a somber hue that will soon pass.

The warmth of Biratnagar's night, with stars that twinkle bright,

Might be the most alluring, a gentle, loving light.

Kathmandu's rain-soaked scent, though lovely, can't compare,

To Biratnagar's balmy evening, beyond the windy air.
