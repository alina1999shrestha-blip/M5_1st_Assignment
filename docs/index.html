In Kathmandu's hazy veil, the air is thick with moisture's scent,

A soothing chill at dawn, a warm caress at eventide's bent.

As warmth abides, a languid breeze that rustles through the heat.

In Aalborg's skies, the sun shines bright, with barely a wisp of grey,

A crisp, cool air that awakens, with a light that chases night's decay.

The cities' contrast beckons, a tale of differing delight,

Aalborg's serene and sunny sky, a haven from the heat of night.
